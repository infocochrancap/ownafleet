The structure

A real business with a real business purpose.

For accelerated depreciation to apply, you need genuine ownership of a real, income-producing asset. The structure below is what makes that defensible — to your CPA, your lender, and the IRS.

01
You own real equipment
Specific, serialized fleet equipment titled to your LLC. Not a fund interest, not pooled, not a security — a real asset you can identify, track, and sell.
02
It produces real income
Your equipment is deployed in a national rental fleet run by a publicly-traded U.S. operating partner. Net fleet rental income is distributed to your LLC monthly.
03
The tax treatment follows
Genuine ownership of real income-producing equipment is what allows the federal tax code's accelerated-depreciation elections to apply. Your CPA confirms scope.
Who it's for

Is this for you?

Most participants come through one of two doors. Either is a fit — specifics get worked out on a call.

Diversifying a windfall

  • A recent capital gain, business sale, or strong investment year
  • Looking for something real — uncorrelated to public markets
  • Want cash flow without operating a business yourself
  • Comfortable holding through the program's contract horizon

Offsetting a high-income year

  • Active business, equity comp, or K-1 generating meaningful taxable income
  • Your CPA has flagged accelerated-depreciation strategies as relevant
  • Open to a multi-year horizon, not a one-off transaction
  • Comfortable with a personal guarantee on financed equipment
This program likely isn't a fit if: you need full liquidity inside a 6-year window · you don't have an income or tax profile that benefits from accelerated depreciation · you'd want investor-style governance rights (this is direct ownership, not a fund interest) · you'd prefer a pooled-vehicle or fund-style structure.
